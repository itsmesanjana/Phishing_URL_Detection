Phishing URL Awareness & Safety Tips

Phishing is a type of cyber attack that tries to steal your sensitive information, such as usernames, passwords, or credit card details, by pretending to be a trustworthy entity. It's essential to be vigilant and follow these safety tips to avoid falling victim to phishing scams.

  • Be cautious with email links: Always verify the sender and never click on suspicious links in emails, even if they look legitimate.
  • Look for HTTPS: Before entering any personal information, make sure the website URL starts with HTTPS and not HTTP.
  • Use two-factor authentication: Enable two-factor authentication on your accounts for an added layer of security.
  • Check website URLs carefully: Make sure the website's URL is spelled correctly and matches the official website you intend to visit.
  • Use a reliable anti-virus program: Install and regularly update anti-virus software to help detect and block phishing attempts.

How to Identify Phishing URLs:

Phishing websites often use URLs that are similar to the official website but have slight differences such as:

  • Misspelled words in the domain name
  • Unusual subdomains, like login.fakebank.com
  • Extra characters or unusual symbols in the URL

Always double-check the URL before you proceed, and if in doubt, do not click on the link.
